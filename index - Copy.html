Welcome to IGSA

Indian Graduate Student Association at Texas A&M University

WHO WE ARE & WHAT WE DO?

IGSA aims to serve Indian graduate students (current, former and future) and their families at Texas A&M University. We work towards the advancement of more than 800 Indian Graduate students. We recognize and understand the unique issues faced by them and help in acclimatizing to College Station. We aim to achieve these through:

  • Advocacy

    Indian graduate students face serious challenges coping with the university requirements. IGSA lends voice to this group of students and promotes involvement at forums aimed towards university-level policy planning efforts. With this aim in mind, IGSA works with agencies like the OGS and GSC, among others, to facilitate their efforts with a focused group of the campus community.

  • Networking

    There is requirement to develop and maintain a strong networking resource that includes members from the current graduate school community, academia, industry professionals and the alumni. It is of utmost importance for the graduate students to be well-informed about the developments beyond their experiences at graduate school and. Through events like socials, information sessions and others, IGSA offers a platform for students to develop a strong network base that is both resourceful as well as engaging.

  • Mentoring

    Incoming Indian graduate students need guidance to step into the daily routines and workings of the department/courses/research. They are unaware of the resources available on campus to help resolve their issues. IGSA provides departmental points-of-contact for all the major departments and schools on campus who can help the students based on their personal experiences. The availability of such mentorship helps the students absorb core Aggie values, which, along with traditional Indian values, help the graduate students become successful Aggies.
